Career and Professional Life of American Actor Tom Atkins

Tom Atkins had no desire to be an actor at first and imagined he would follow in his father’s footsteps. However, after graduating from college, he began his career in stage plays, both on and off Broadway.

He later relocated to Los Angeles to seek a career in cinema and television. And “The Detective” was his debut film role. After appearing in TV shows and movies, including as Lt. Alex Diel in seasons 1 and 2 of “The Rockford Files,” Atkins transitioned into the horror and science fiction genres.

He went on to appear in two films, “The Fog” (1980) and “Escape from New York” (1981). He received a lot of accolades for his performance and role in both of these horror movies. The Carpenter-produced “Halloween III: Season of the Witch” in 1982 was his next big role.

He also got the opportunity to collaborate on three distinct projects with George A. Romero. Stephen King’s anthology picture Creepshow, released in 1982, was one of them. They went on to work together on “Two Evil Eyes” in 1990. They also collaborated on the film “Bruiser” in 2000.

More on Career

Similarly, he adored his portrayal of Detective Ray Cameron in the 1986 horror thriller “Night of the Creeps.” He has also created a reputation for himself in the thriller and police procedural genres. His performance as Michael Hunsaker in the 1987 film “Lethal Weapon” was one of his most well-known.

In 1993, Atkins appeared in the film “Striking Distance.” Furthermore, he is not restricted to films; in the 1990s, he repeated his role as Commander Diehl in a series of “Rockford Files” movies on television.

Tom Atkins is a skilled actor who has built a name for himself in the Pittsburgh theater scene. And, at the Pittsburgh Public Theater, he gave a memorable performance in the one-man show “The Chief.” He went on to play Art Rooney, the late founder of the Pittsburgh Steelers, in that episode. He also had the chance to play Macbeth, alongside Jean Smart as Lady Macbeth and Keith Fowler as Macduff.

Atkins also appeared in A Musical Christmas Carol at the Pittsburgh Civic Light Opera as Ebenezer Scrooge. In 1973, he received the “Drama Desk Award for Most Promising Performer” for his appearance on “Broadway” in The Changing Room.

Then, in 2009, he played a retired sheriff in the remake “My Bloody Valentine 3D” and co-starred with Nicolas Cage in “Todd Farmer’s Drive Angry” in 2011. Furthermore, he played Professor Westlake, who examined the biology of the extraterrestrial lifeform in the 2018 sci-fi film Encounter.
