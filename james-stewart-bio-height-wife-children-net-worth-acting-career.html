James Stewart Bio, Height, Wife, Children, Net Worth, Acting Career

Some films will always resonate with American audiences, regardless of new blockbuster hits and cinematic advancements. One such film is the Christmas classic It’s a Wonderful Life, a film in which James Stewart is most remembered. James also introduced the military aviation film Strategic Air Command which was released by Paramount Pictures in 1955.

Besides acting, James Stewart had a distinguished career as a military man during the Vietnam War and World War II. He rose to the rank of Brigadier General and so far holds the record for the highest player in Miltary.

The biography of James Stewart

Born on May 20, 1908 in Indiana, Pennsylvania, James Stewart’s father, Alexander Maitland Stewart, was an American of Scottish ancestry, while James was the mother of Elizabeth Ruth, a talented pianist and homemaker.

The Stewart family was a Presbyterian household that had three children of which James was the eldest sibling. He had two younger sisters – Mary Kelly Stewart and Virginia Wilson Stewart. James graduated from Mercersburg Academy Preparatory School in 1928. During his studies, he participated in many extracurricular activities. He was on the choir club, the John Marshall Literary Society, the football team, and the school track team where he was a high jumper under coach Jimmy Curran.

As a child, James dreamed of becoming a pilot, so when he enrolled at Princeton University to study architecture, it was no surprise that his final thesis focused on airport designs. At Princeton, he also discovered he had the potential to make it as an actor and soon began to land minor roles in theatrical performances.

His acting career was punctuated by his commitments in the army. He enlisted in the armed forces in 1941 as an air corps, where he logged over 1,800 flying hours as a bomber pilot.

Over a career spanning six decades, James Stewart received five Academy Award nominations, received an Academy Lifetime Achievement Award in 1985, and was named the third greatest male legend of the Golden Age of Hollywood by the American Film Institute.

Acting career

Initially, James’ ambitions were not slanted, but as a student at Princeton he became more and more interested in the school’s drama and literature club. His first appearance was in a Broadway play called Porter Nation after which he had a cameo appearance in Goodbye Again

His career began with a movie deal with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer in 1934. His first film was The Murderer which was shot in 1935. His next major work was 1938 ‘s You Can’t Take It With You and Mr. Smith se travels to Washington which was shot the following year. Mr. Smith’s trip to Washington gave him his first Oscar nomination. He finally won an Oscar the following year (1940) for the classic, The Philadelphia Story

Between 1940 and 1945, he experienced a career break due to his involvement in the war. He made a return to acting in 1946 in It’s a Wonderful Life for which he received another Oscar nomination.

Over the course of his career, James Stewart has forged an on-screen identity for his serene demeanor and calm portrayal of America’s middle-class man. Over the next few decades of his life he produced masterpieces including The Man Who Knew Too Much (1956) and Rear Window (1954), Vertigo (1958) and Winchester ’73. His last role was in the animated comedy game An American Tail: Fievel Heads West which was shot in 1991.

James Stewart died on July 2, 1997, at his home in Beverly Hills, California, at the age of 89, after a series of health problems.

Net Worth – What Was James Stewart Worth?

James Stewart by all accounts had a glittering career spanning over 60 years. During his lifetime, he was part of many commercially viable films, including his role in the sentimental 1946 holiday favorite, It’s a Wonderful Life. Other commercially successful projects included the 1965 Civil War film Shenandoah. He also made a sizable income from endorsements and advertisements. At the time he was laid to rest, Stewart was worth around $30 million.

Woman, children

In his early days in Hollywood, James earned a casanova reputation alongside his friend Henry Fonda. Returning from World War II, aged 41, he married former model Gloria Hatrick McLean on August 9, 1949. Gloria had two sons from a previous marriage – Michael and Ronald. Stewart adopted the two and with Gloria had twin daughters, named Judy and Kelly. The twins were born on May 7, 1951. Kelly is an evolutionary anthropologist. James’ stepson Ronald lost his life in battle while serving in the United States Marine Corps in 1969.

James and Gloria were married until February 16, 1994, when Gloria lost her battle with lung cancer at the age of 75.
