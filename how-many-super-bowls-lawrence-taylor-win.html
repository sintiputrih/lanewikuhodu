How many Super Bowls Lawrence Taylor win?

Lawrence Taylor, a legendary linebacker in the NFL, had an illustrious career that included multiple Super Bowl victories. Let's delve into the details of his Super Bowl triumphs and his remarkable achievements in the NFL.

Super Bowl XXI Victory

In Super Bowl XXI, which took place on January 25, 1987, Lawrence Taylor's team, the New York Giants, faced off against the Denver Broncos. The Giants emerged victorious with a score of 39-20, securing Taylor's first Super Bowl win. Taylor's exceptional performance during this game was highlighted by a key touchdown preventing tackle on a goal-line play in the first half, where he stopped Broncos quarterback John Elway as he sprinted out on a rollout.

Super Bowl XXV Triumph

Taylor's second Super Bowl victory came in Super Bowl XXV on January 27, 1991, when the Giants clashed with the Buffalo Bills. The Giants secured a narrow victory with a score of 20-19, after Buffalo's Scott Norwood missed a potential game-winning field goal in the closing seconds of the game. Notably, Taylor played a crucial role in the lead-up to this victory, contributing three sacks and a forced fumble against the Eagles in the playoffs.

Additional Achievements

Beyond his Super Bowl victories, Lawrence Taylor's career was studded with remarkable accomplishments:

  • He was named the NFL Defensive Rookie of the Year in 1981.

  • Taylor was honored as the NFL Defensive Player of the Year three times, in 1981, 1982, and 1986.

  • He was selected for the Pro Bowl an impressive 10 times.

  • Taylor's outstanding performance led to him being unanimously voted the league's MVP in 1986, a rare feat for a defensive player.

Lawrence Taylor's legacy in the NFL is defined not only by his exceptional skill as a linebacker but also by his pivotal role in securing two Super Bowl victories for the New York Giants. His impact on the field and his numerous accolades solidify his status as one of the most iconic figures in the history of American football.
