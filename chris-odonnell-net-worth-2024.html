Early Life and Career Beginnings

Chris O’Donnell was born in Winnetka, Illinois, and attended Loyola Academy and Boston College before embarking on his acting career. His first significant role was in the movie “Men Don’t Leave” in 1990, which marked the beginning of his journey in the entertainment industry.

Rise to Stardom

O’Donnell’s breakthrough came when he was cast as Charlie Simms in “Scent of a Woman” alongside Al Pacino. His performance earned him a nomination for the Golden Globe Award. He further gained recognition for his roles in “Circle of Friends” and “Batman Forever.”

Television Career

In addition to his film career, O’Donnell has also made a name for himself on television. He is best known for his role as Special Agent G. Callen on the CBS crime drama “NCIS: Los Angeles,” a role he has been playing since 2009.

Net Worth from Acting

Endorsements and Other Ventures
