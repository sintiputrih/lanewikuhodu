Who Was Kyle Jacobs?

American country music composer, singer, guitarist, and pianist Kyle Christopher Jacobs (June 26, 1973 – February 17, 2023), who had worked for Curb Music as a staff writer since 2003, passed away on February 17, 2023. Jacobs composed piano and guitar pieces. His roots are in the Minnesota city of Bloomington.

Husband and wife duo Jacobs and Kellie Pickler
Jacobs collaborated with Garth Brooks on the song “More Than a Memory,” which became the first single to ever enter at the top of Billboard’s Country Singles chart. Many musicians, including Kimberley Locke (who recorded his co-written “8th World Wonder”) and Trace Adkins, Jo Dee Messina, Craig Morgan, Tim McGraw, Kellie Pickler (his wife), Clay Walker, Kelly Clarkson, and Scotty McCreery, have recorded songs written by Jacobs. As a composer and musician, Jacobs had worked with the likes of Darius Rucker, Rachel Thibodeau, and Wynonna.

He co-starred with Pickler in the CMT docuseries I Love Kellie Pickler.

After two and a half years of dating, country music star Kellie Pickler announced her engagement to Jacobs on June 23, 2010. Jacobs proposed to Pickler on June 15, 2010, the day of her late grandmother’s birthday, while they were on a beach at sunset. Pickler and Jacobs tied the knot on New Year’s Day, 2011. Jacobs also helped start a youth group called UTEC (United Teenagers Experience Christ) in the Twin Cities area of Minnesota.

A suicide was the cause of Jacobs’ death on February 17, 2023 in Nashville, Tennessee. He was 49.
