Dominik Livaković Biography

Soccer player Dominik Livakovic is well-known. His birthday is January 9, 1995, and he was born in Zadar, Croatia. Dominik is also known as a goalkeeper who rose to prominence while playing for Dinamo Zagreb in the Croatian Prva HNL. In 2016-2017, he helped the team win the Prva HNL Championship. Dominik is a Croatian native. He and Marcelo Brozovic were teammates for Croatia’s national soccer team at the 2018 FIFA World Cup.

Career

  • Livakovi was a part of NK Zagreb’s first squad at the start of the 2012-13 season. Initially the club’s backup goalkeeper, he made his league debut on August 31, 2012, when he played the full game against HNK Cibalia in a 1-0 defeat at home. He swiftly progressed to first-team status for the club, appearing in 104 league matches in four seasons, including 90 in the Prva HNL.
  • On August 30, 2015, he agreed to join Dinamo Zagreb for the start of the 2016-17 season. He made his league debut for the club on October 2, 2016, in a goalless draw at home against Hajduk Split. On October 18, 2016, he made his UEFA Champions League debut, losing 1-0 at home to Sevilla.
  • On 30 July 2019, Livakovi broke Drain Ladi’s record of most minutes from the start of a Dinamo season without conceding a goal (413), set in 1995, in a 3-0 Champions League qualification victory against Saburtalo Tbilisi. Three days later, he surrendered his first goal of the season in a 3-1 league win against Gorica, establishing a new record of 535 goals.
  • On August 26, 2020, Livakovi stopped Ciprian Deac’s penalty shot in a Champions League qualifier against CFR Cluj. The game was tied 2-2 after extra time, prompting a penalty shootout. Dinamo won the shootout 6-5 and progressed to the third qualifying round when Livakovi made a crucial stop on Cătălin Golofca’s attempt. Livakovi was praised for his performance in the 2020-21 Europa League group stage, as he surrendered just one goal in five games while leading Dinamo to the first position. On October 22, he notably stopped Steven Berghuis’ penalty attempt in Dinamo’s 0-0 tie with Feyenoord.
  • On March 18, 2021, when the score was 3-0 for Dinamo, Livakovi stopped Harry Kane’s sitter in the Europa League Round of 16 against Tottenham Hotspur, resulting in a 3-2 aggregate win and the club’s first Europa League quarter-final.
  • Livakovi gained his first call-up to the Croatian national team in May 2016 for a friendly against Moldova. He made his debut against Chile in the 2017 China Cup, in which Croatia lost on penalties. In May 2018, he was included in Croatia’s provisional 32-man squad for the 2018 FIFA World Cup in Russia, and he was later a part of the team that finished second after losing the final to France.
  • He made his competitive debut against England on October 12, 2018, in a scoreless draw at Stadion Rujevica in Rijeka during the 2018-19 Nations League match. Following the underwhelming performances of Lovre Kalini, Livakovi was designated Croatia’s first-choice goalkeeper for the UEFA Euro 2020 qualifying campaign, which kicks off on June 8, 2019, against Wales.
