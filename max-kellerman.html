Max Kellerman - Bio, Age, Net Worth, Height, Single, Career, Facts

Table of Biography

Max Kellerman is an American sports television personality. Max Kellerman is mostly popular for his boxing commentary on HBO World Championship Boxing and HBO Boxing After Dark. Currently, he works as the co-host of the ESPN talk show First Take.

Early Life and Childhood

Max Kellerman was born on 6th August 1973 and is currently 50 years old. He was born in The Bronx and grew up in Greenwich Village with his family. He was the eldest among the 4 brothers in the Kellerman family.

However, his younger brother Sam was murdered On October 17, 2004. Former boxer James Butler is behind the murder.

Career and Professional Life

Sports Broadcasting

As a teenager, Kellerman had started his broadcasting journey. Back then he hosted a TV program called Max on Boxing on a New York City public-access television cable. However, his career journey took a step up after graduation.

In the late 90s, ESPN hired Kellerman as an analyst on its boxing series Friday Night Fights. After working as an analyst for a few years, he got his show Around the Horn in November 2002.

Although the show was an incredible hit, Killman and ESPN could not reach an agreement. Consequently, he left the network in 2004.

From there he moved to host I, on Fox Sports Net with Michael Holley and Bill Wolff. Despite gathering the highest ranking the show was canceled the following year. Later he signed with MSNBC as a permanent contributor on its show Tucker.

Apart from this Kellerman also cohosted Spike TV’s 2006 series, King of Vegas.
