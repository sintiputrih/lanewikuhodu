Sergio Busquets is a Spanish professional footballer who is best known for his career as a defensive midfielder and captain of the La Liga club Barcelona as well as the Spain national team. He is considered as one of the best midfielders of all time and has played for teams like Badia, Barberà Andalucía, Jàbac Terrassa, and Lleida. During the course of his career, Sergio has won numerous tournaments such as La Liga, Copa del Rey, Supercopa de España, FIFA Club World Cup, UEFA Super Cup, FIFA World Cup, UEFA European Championship, and has been the recipient of honors like La Liga‘s “Breakthrough Player” and UEFA Nations League Finals‘ “Player of the Tournament”.

    Sergio Busquets Facts

  • Sergio started playing football in a local football club called CD Badia del Valles.
  • During his youth career, Busquets played for UE Lleida, CEF Barbera Andalucia, UFB Jabac Terrassa, and FC Barcelona.
  • In 2006, Sergio played for Barcelona C where he stayed until 2007 when he shifted to Barcelona B.
  • Busquets became a part of the first team of FC Barcelona in 2008.
  • On December 22, 2008, he decided to extend his contract with the Spanish club until 2013. His contract included a buy-out clause with a price of 80 million euros.
  • He achieved his first La Liga goal in a 2-0 success over Athletic Bilbao on March 7, 2009.
  • On January 27, 2011, Busquets extended his deal with Barcelona until 2015. His buyout clause rose to 150 million euros.
  • On July 16, 2013, Sergio again extended his contract with the Spanish team until 2018.
  • After Carles Puyol retired on August 1, 2014, Busquets wore the number 5 shirt for 2014–2015 La Liga season.
  • During the Euro 2016 qualifier, he scored his first international goal in a 5-1 victory over Macedonia.
  • Busquets won the Bravo Award in 2009.
  • Sergio was part of the UEFA Champions League Team for the 2014-2015 season.
  • As a player of F.C Barcelona, Sergio won the 2008-2009, 2010-2011, and 2014-2015 UEFA Champions League.
  • Busquets was part of the Spanish national team which won the 2010 FIFA World Cup.
