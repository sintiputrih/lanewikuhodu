Charlie Day Parents: Meet Thomas C. Day and Mary Day

Charlie Day is an American actor, comedian, writer, and producer. Day is best known for his role as Charlie Kelly on the hit TV show “It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia,” which he also co-created, co-wrote, and produced.

Day has been recognized for his work with multiple award nominations, including two Critics’ Choice Television Awards and a Screen Actors Guild Award for his work on “It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia.”

He has also been involved in philanthropy, supporting causes such as the United Service Organizations (USO) and the Innocence Project.

A skilled musician, Day can play the piano, accordion, trombone, guitar, and harmonica, and has written or improvised most of the songs featured on It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia. In 2014, he received an honorary doctorate in performing arts from Merrimack College, where he also delivered that year’s keynote address.

Charlie Day Parents: Meet Thomas C. Day and Mary Day

Charlie Day’s mother’s name is Mary and his father’s name is Thomas Charles Day. His mother worked as a piano teacher at The Pennfield School in Portsmouth, Rhode Island. She was of English descent.

His father was a music professor at Salve Regina University in Newport, Rhode Island. Charlie was raised in a musical family and learned to play the piano at a young age.

He is the youngest of two children, with an elder sister named Alice. Charlie Day has spoken in interviews about his close relationship with his parents and how they have supported his career.

In fact, his father even appeared on an episode of “It’s Always Sunny in Philadelphia” playing a role in one of the episodes.
