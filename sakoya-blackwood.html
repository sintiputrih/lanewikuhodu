All About Sakoya Blackwood Who Blackmailed AMC CEO

Sakoya Blackwood (age 35, born in 1988) is a Bronx con artist who blackmailed wealthy men and tried extracting six-figure amounts from her victims. In 2022, Sakoya was arrested after she started blackmailing AMC’s CEO Adam Aron.

In March 2022, a woman named Blackwood started a deceptive scheme by texting AMC’s Adam Aron. Posing as a 17-year-old ballet dancer through fake pictures of a Russian model, she misled Aron, who believed she was someone from his past. Their messages escalated, and Aron ended up sharing explicit photos, including ones with another woman.

Blackwood invented fake characters, masquerading as an ex-boyfriend and a Vanity Fair reporter, claiming they had seen the messages. She demanded money to keep the matter hidden, threatening to expose the explicit content to the media and AMC’s board. Aron, however, stood strong and reported the blackmail to the FBI.

Subsequently, the FBI swiftly acted, leading to the arrest of Sakoya Blackwood on August 31, 2023. The court proceedings unveiled Blackwood’s use of multiple false online identities to deceive and extort wealthy individuals.

These days this case is being talked about because Aron posted a tweet on his Twitter handle explaining what happened to him and how he was targeted and blackmailed.

How did Sakoya Set a Trap For Adam Aron?

Investigation and Trial

Aron stayed strong and didn’t give in to the blackmail. He told the FBI about what happened. On October 12, 2023, the CEO shared this on Twitter, saying how important it is for people with power to stand up against blackmail.

After Aron told the FBI, they quickly started looking into it and found the person responsible, Sakoya Blackwood. She was arrested on August 31, 2023. They took her to court in Manhattan, where Judge Jennifer Willis and Judge Jesse M. Furman oversaw the case.

In court, they discovered that Blackwood used fake identities online to trick rich and famous people and get money from them. She made a deal with the court to avoid a long 27-year sentence. Instead, she got credit for the time she had already spent in jail, which ended in July.
