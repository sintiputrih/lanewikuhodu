Carlos Mencia | Career

Carlos started his career in 1988 when he changed his name. He took the suggestion from the comedy store owner Mitzi Shore to change his first name. Mencia did so that he could appeal to Mexican audiences.

Eventually, Carlos made his T.V. debut with an uncredited role on In Living Color in 1990. Followed it with a small role on An Evening at the Improv in 1992, Comic Relief VI, and HBO Comedy Half-Hour in 1994.

Talking about his voice-acting career, Carlos voiced on the series The Proud Family from 2001 to 2005. He reiterated the role in The Proud Family Movie in 2005.

Carlos has also appeared in multiple movie projects, including Outta Time, Drawn Together, Farce of the Penguins, 29 Palms, The Heartbreak Kid, and Our Family Wedding.

He has received a NAMIC Vision Award nomination and two ALMA Award nominations for Outstanding Male Performance for Best Performance – Comedy.
