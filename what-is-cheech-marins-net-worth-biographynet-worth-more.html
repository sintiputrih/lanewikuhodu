Who is Cheech Marin?

Richard Anthony “Cheech” Marin was born on July 13, 1946 in South Los Angeles, California, United States. He is the son of Elsa Meza, a secretary and Oscar Marin, a police officer for the LAPD, both of whom are Mexican-Americans. When Cheech was a child, he underwent surgery to repair a cleft lip, and he got the nickname “Cheech” as a baby when his uncle said he looked like a chicharrón (fried pork rind).

Marin and family relocated to Granada Hills, California in 1955. He went to St. John Baptist de la Salle Catholic School for his primary education. He then attended Bishop Alemany High School. During his teenage years, he started attending folk music events at the Ash Grove on Melrose Avenue. Marin enrolled to California State University, Northridge which was at the time known as San Fernando Valley State College and was a member of Phi Sigma Kappa fratenity.

To avoid being drafted into the Vietnam War, Marin moved to Vancouver, British Columbia in the late 1960s. It was there he met Tommy Chong and the pair began performing together in an improv troupe. They later teamed up as a stand-up comedy duo, releasing their debut album, “Cheech and Chong” in 1971.
