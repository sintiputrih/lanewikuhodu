Shoppers With Sun-Damaged Skin Love This Retinol Cream

At 30, I’ve finally incorporated retinol into my skincare routine — and can confirm it’s worth the hype. I have dry skin — and had, in light of this fact, avoided the ingredient for years, afraid I’d exacerbate my already-parched complexion.

A recent retinol-spiked product discovery, however, enlightened me: Non-drying, sensitive-skin-friendly retinol formulas do, in fact, exist. The Cosrx 0.1% Retinol Cream — hailing from the Korean brand behind the snail mucin serum you’ve undoubtedly seen on social media — is among them, per shoppers. The formula is equal parts effective and gentle; and, best of all, it’s a mere $13 for a limited time. 

The Cosrx 0.1% Retinol Cream is an anti-aging treatment that stars its titular ingredient. The layerable, lightweight formula harnesses the power of the vitamin A derivative to fade fine lines, resurface skin, and deliver a generally more youthful appearance — with minimal irritation, even for first time retinol users. In fact, it’s gentle enough to use around the eye area, in addition to skin on the neck, chest, and face.

A quick retinol refresh: The ingredient, a derivative of vitamin A, improves skin-cell turnover — in turn, delivering a smoother skin surface and fewer visible lines. “The science behind…retinol is complex and involves multiple cellular processes," cosmetic chemist Yashi Shrestha previously told InStyle; "However, the result is a significant improvement in the appearance and health of the skin." 

While it does deliver a bounty of benefits, the ingredient is also known to induce dryness and flakiness as skin acclimates. The Cosrx retinol, however, contains ingredients to counterbalance dryness, making it an ideal option for anyone who’d prefer to minimize — or completely skip — the potential parched-skin stage.

Specifically, you’ll find two hydrating skin-soothers, panthenol and vitamin E, which can help mitigate retinol’s potentially drying effects. Panthenol, aka B5, traps moisture in the skin to impart long-lasting hydration. Vitamin E, a skin-softening antioxidant, supports suppleness and smoothness. Furthermore, it fends off environmental skin agers, such as pollution, to support a more youthful-looking, even-toned complexion. 

Shoppers say the formula works as promised — with “no irritation,” according to one. Another reviewer, a 42-year-old whose “years of sunbathing” left them with signs of premature skin aging, says the cream is “a wonderful alternative to microdermabrasion” thanks to its smoothing, skin-resurfacing effects. 

For a line-fighting, complexion-resurfacing solution that stars skincare’s preeminent anti-aging ingredient, shop the Cosrx 0.1% Retinol Cream at Amazon while it’s over 50 percent off, at a mere $13, for a limited time.
