Here’s all you need to know about Tucker Carlson’s early life

On May 16, 1969, in San Francisco, California, the United States, Tucker Carlson was born. He is the older son of Lisa McNear, a native of San Francisco, and Dick Carlson, a former “gonzo reporter” who went on to become the director of Voice of America, president of the Corporation for Public Broadcasting, and the American ambassador to the Seychelles.

The name of Tucker’s younger brother is Buckley Peck Carlson. His younger sibling Buckley has experience as a Republican political operative and communications manager. Carlson has a quarter-sixteenth Swiss-Italian, English, and German ancestry.

In 1976, Tucker’s parents divorced after nine years of marriage. Tucker and his younger brother Buckley were given to his father’s custody. When he was 6 years old, his mother abandoned her children and the family to live a bohemian existence.

When did Tucker Carlson start his media career?

In the 1990s, Carlson began his media career by contributing to The Weekly Standard and other publications. He provided commentary at CNN from 2000 to 2005. He presided over MSNBC’s nightly programme Tucker from 2005 until 2008. Along with this, he spent two seasons as the anchor of Tucker Carlson: Unfiltered on PBS.

2009 saw Tucker begin his employment with Fox News. He frequently appeared as a guest panellist on the network’s late-night satire programme Red Eye with Greg Gutfeld during his early tenure there. He soon founded and held the position of editor-in-chief of The Daily Caller, a news website that he started in 2010.

Since the year 2016, Tucker Carlson has hosted the programme Tucker Carlson Tonight. “Tucker Carlson Tonight is the sworn adversary of lying, pomposity, smugness, and group think,” the website’s description of the programme states.
