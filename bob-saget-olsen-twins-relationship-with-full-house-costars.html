As a result, fans quickly came to love the series’ stars which is why fans still want to know what happened to Full House’s cast after the series ended.

John Stamos’ Shocking Joke About Bob Saget And Mary-Kate Olsen

When Bob Saget rose to fame, his image seemed incredibly squeaky clean at first. After all, on top of starring in arguably the most innocent sitcom on television, Full House, Saget also hosted America's Funniest Home Videos. During each episode of America's Funniest Home Videos, Saget made a series of silly faces and put on ridiculous voices as he narrated the amusing clips.

Due to his television persona, many fans were completely shocked when they learned how raunchy Bob Saget’s standup comedy was. Since it had become common knowledge that Saget had a dark and perverse sense of humor, however, it made sense when it was announced that he was going to be the subject of a comedy roast. Even though every Comedy Central Roast features over-the-top remarks, Saget’s roast featured some extremely disturbing jokes.

The Olsen Twins Relationship With Their Full House Co-Stars

When Full House was at the height of its popularity, everyone knew that Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen were the show’s most popular stars. As a result of that, once Full House came to an end, the Olsen Twins were able to become extremely wealthy after building a brand that marketed things to their millions of fans. After attaining all of that success, however, the Olsen twins notably pulled back from the spotlight and now it is widely believed that they hate being famous.

Given the fact that they have been under the harsh spotlight of the press all of their lives, most people assume that the tabloids are the only reason why Mary-Kate and Ashley Olsen hate fame. However, some people have concluded that the Olsen twins have another reason to loathe fame. After all, at times it has seemed like there might be tensions between the Olsens and their former Full House co-stars.

When Full House was revived for the sequel series Fuller House, almost everyone who starred in the original series returned to their roles. However, throughout Fuller House’s five-season run, the Olsen twins never made an appearance. Instead, the only way that the Olsen twins were a part of Fuller House is the show occasionally made thinly veiled references to their refusal to return to their roles.

Given the fact that many Full House fans wanted to see the Olsen Twins reunite with their former Full House co-stars, it makes sense that they read a lot into Fuller House’s references to the sisters. However, there are many, many examples of Fuller House’s stars defending the Olsens’ decision to not take part in the revival. On top of that, the Olsens’ former childhood co-stars have expressed their love for the duo and when Bob Saget passed away, Mary-Kate and Ashley made it clear they mourned his demise. With all of that in mind, it seems clear that while the Olsens seem to hate fame, they love their former Full House co-stars.
