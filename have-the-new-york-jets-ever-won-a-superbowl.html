Have the New York Jets Ever won a Superbowl?

The New York Jets are a professional American football team based in the New York metropolitan area. The New York Jets were established in 1959 as the New York Titans and played their home games at the Polo Grounds. However, the team struggled financially and changed ownership in 1963. The new owner, Sonny Werblin, renamed the team the New York Jets.

The New York Jets' only Super Bowl victory came in Super Bowl III, which was played on January 12, 1969. The game was held at the Miami Orange Bowl and featured a matchup between the Jets and the Baltimore Colts.

The Jets, led by Namath, were considered heavy underdogs going into the game. However, Namath famously guaranteed a victory before the game, and his words proved prophetic. The Jets defeated the Colts by a score of 16-7, with Namath being named the game's Most Valuable Player (MVP).
